    National Genomics Data Center (NGDC)

    Database

    国家基因组科学数据中心

    DESCRIPTION
    Operated by CNCB (China National Center for Bioinformation). The NGDC provides open-access data and services for life and health sciences—supporting translation of large-scale data into discoveries for academia and industry. The site describes the center as National Genomics Data Center (formerly the BIG Data Center) and highlights national-scale infrastructure (multi-petabyte storage, high-throughput compute, and wide network capacity). Flagship sequence services center on GSA (Genome Sequence Archive) and Genome Warehouse (GWH); other prominent portals include GenBase (genetic sequences), GVM (Genome Variation Map), OMIX (miscellaneous multi-omics), BioProject and BioSample libraries, GSA-Human (human omics with controlled access), MethBank, GEN (Gene Expression Nebulas), Database Commons (biological database catalog), BioCode, BIT (Bioinformatics Toolkit), OpenLB, and BIG Search (cross-domain search). The English hub also groups partner access to EBI- and NCBI-family databases alongside NGDC-hosted resources.
    GSA
    基因组序列存档
    https://ngdc.cncb.ac.cn/gsa/
    Genome Sequence Archive—repository for raw sequencing reads, assemblies, and study metadata at NGDC, in the same role as SRA / ENA within the INSDC ecosystem.
    GSA-Human
    人类基因组序列存档
    https://ngdc.cncb.ac.cn/gsa-human/
    Human-focused omics archive with controlled access and policy-driven release, analogous in purpose to controlled human read archives elsewhere.
    OMIX
    多组学数据存档
    https://ngdc.cncb.ac.cn/omix/
    Multi-omics submission and browse portal for datasets that do not fit a single “sequence-only” archive—metadata-rich miscellaneous omics at NGDC.
    URL
    https://ngdc.cncb.ac.cn/?lang=en

    CNGBdb (CNGB)

    Database

    国家基因库数据库

    DESCRIPTION
    Unified portal for sharing and using biological data, built on large-scale data, cloud computing, and AI. DNA Omics, Cell Omics, and Spatial Omics hubs surface 40+ scientific databases spanning biodiversity, microbiology, and health. Core services include CNSA (China National GeneBank Sequence Archive) for omics submission via the CNSA submit portal, spatial-omics submission, FTP-based dataset download, BLAST and other analysis tools, bioinformatics training, and cooperation offerings (database development, hosting, and analysis). The site publishes handbooks plus terms, privacy, and data-sharing policies.
    URL
    https://db.cngb.org/
    Also known as
    China National GeneBank Database

    National Bioscience Database Center (NBDC)

    Database
    DESCRIPTION
    NBDC coordinates Japanese life-science database policy and infrastructure with domestic and international partners. It oversees human data sharing (review, security guidelines, and linkage to archives such as JGA), group-level restricted sharing, and discovery services such as TogoVar. Day-to-day operation of the NBDC Human Database and NBDC Group Shared Database platforms is carried out with DBCLS in cooperation with the DDBJ Center at NIG.
    NBDC Human Database
    https://humandbs.dbcls.jp/en
    Portal to apply for submission or reuse of human omics and related data under NBDC guidelines; reviews controlled-access datasets archived in JGA and coordinates with DDBJ. (Operated by DBCLS; successor site to the former *humandbs.biosciencedbc.jp* URL.)
    JGA
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/jga/
    Japanese Genotype-phenotype Archive—repository for individual-level genetic and phenotypic data with access controls; Japan’s counterpart in spirit to dbGaP and the EGA, operated by the DDBJ Center under NBDC/DBCLS policy workflows.
    NBDC Group Shared Database
    https://gr-sharingdbs.dbcls.jp/en
    Infrastructure for laboratory- and consortium-scale sharing when public release or JGA-style archives are not appropriate—group-restricted access with NBDC oversight. (Operated by DBCLS; replaces the former *gr-sharingdbs.biosciencedbc.jp* service.)
    TogoVar
    https://togovar.org/
    Japanese genetic variation browser integrating allele frequencies, transcripts, and disease/clinical annotations—useful for interpreting variants in Japanese and related cohorts; currently provided via DBCLS (historically promoted through NBDC).
    URL
    https://biosciencedbc.jp/en/

    DNA Data Bank of Japan (DDBJ)

    Database
    DESCRIPTION
    The DDBJ Center at the National Institute of Genetics (NIG) operates Japan’s INSDC member services: the DDBJ nucleotide archive, DRA (raw reads), GEA (expression), and BioProject / BioSample metadata, with unified submission through D-way. Data flow is synchronized with NCBI and ENA; JGA for controlled human data is operated alongside NBDC human-data policies.
    DDBJ
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/ddbj/index-e.html
    Annotated and assembled nucleotide sequences (INSDC); accessions and daily exchange with GenBank and ENA keep a single global nucleotide corpus.
    DRA
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/dra/index-e.html
    DDBJ Sequence Read Archive—raw sequencing reads, metadata, and experiment records; INSDC partner of SRA and ENA under common run accession conventions.
    GEA
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/gea/index-e.html
    Genomic Expression Archive—submissions from microarray and high-throughput sequencing (e.g. RNA-seq, ChIP-seq) with MIAME/MINSEQE-style metadata; complements GEO-style discovery.
    BioProject
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/bioproject/index-e.html
    Study-level registration for sequencing and functional projects; links to BioSample, DRA, DDBJ, and GEA objects so INSDC submissions stay traceable end to end.
    BioSample
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/biosample/index-e.html
    Sample-level metadata (organism, tissue, collection) shared across INSDC; sample accessions tie experiments in DRA and GEA to reproducible biological context.
    JGA
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/jga/
    Japanese Genotype-phenotype Archive—controlled-access storage for human genotype and phenotype data; submission and data use are gated through the NBDC Human Database.
    URL
    https://www.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/index-e.html
