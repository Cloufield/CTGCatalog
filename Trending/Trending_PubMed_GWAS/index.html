    Trending PubMed GWAS

    Topic trends: track how themes in the literature rise or fall over time; this page applies that idea to GWAS-related PubMed indexing—counts by year, keyword bucket, and journal.

    PubMed baseline snapshot: indexed article counts for GWAS-related MeSH and title/abstract buckets, plus journal rankings (volume and share), for publication years 2007–2026.

    Provenance

    • Baseline files merged: 1334
    • Distinct journals (any row): 23039
    • Share ranking includes only journals with at least one GWAS-related hit and ≥ 50 articles in the window (overall) or in that calendar year (by-year tables).
    • Regenerate this page after re-running scripts/pubmed_baseline_gwas.py and scripts/render_trending_pubmed_gwas.py.
    • Interactive asset: docs/assets/trending/pubmed_gwas_dashboard.html (regenerated with this script).

    Methods

    Counts are derived offline from NCBI PubMed baseline Medline XML (gzip shards such as pubmed*.xml.gz), streamed with xml.etree.ElementTree.iterparse so memory stays bounded; optional workers process files in parallel and results are merged from per-file JSON shards.

    Publication year comes from JournalIssue.PubDate (or ArticleDate when needed). Journal is keyed by MedlineJournalInfo.MedlineTA when present, otherwise ISO abbreviation or full journal title.

    Trend buckets (per year): Step (1) — MeSH_GWAS (MeSH descriptor contains “genome-wide association”) and text_GWAS_core (GWAS / genome-wide association text) are counted whenever they match. Step (2) — every other text bucket (text_PRS, text_MR, omics layers, fine-mapping, etc.) is counted only if the article is GWAS-related (same rule as journal rankings: MeSH_GWAS or text_GWAS_core). All text buckets use case-insensitive regexes over title, abstract, and author keywords. An article can contribute to more than one bucket; subtopic lines are conditional on the GWAS gate as above.

    Journal GWAS-related counts use a single article-level flag: MeSH_GWAS or text_GWAS_core only (PRS/MR text alone does not count toward journal volume/share). Share rankings restrict to journals with at least one such hit and a minimum article count in the window (overall or per calendar year, as noted above).
